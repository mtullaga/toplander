Hearing loss can lead to loss of balance and falls too

According to Dr. Frank Lin of John Hopkins, as you walk your ears pick up subtle cues that help with balance.(2)

Hearing loss mutes these important signals.

The ear of course is not only responsible for hearing but for balance too. This is called the vestibular system which is in the inner ear.

Dr. Lin conducted a study that showed even mild hearing loss can increase your risk of a fall by 3 times!

And this falling risk may rise by 140% with every additional 10 decibels of hearing loss.(3)

So your hearing loss can lead to nasty falls that can leave your body broken and riddled with pain.

Falls can even be deadly. Thats why 32,000 seniors die from falls every year.

One-fourth of seniors who fracture a hip from a fall will die within six months of the injury.(4)

Thats why so many doctors recommend hearing aids to people with balance issues.

How Does  The Atom OTC Hearing Aids Help With Tinnitus? 

Im glad you asked.

Medical science still doesnt have a firm grasp on what causes tinnitus. It's still a mystery in many ways.

Many researchers believe that nearly everyone has some version of tinnitus.

Ask anyone to sit quietly in a silent room and theyll hear the ringing, buzzing or clicking that we think of as tinnitus.

But folks with hearing loss hear tinnitus much louder.

Audiologists believe that the always there ringing sounds louder because the rest of the world is turned down by hearing loss.

The ringing is just far more noticeable when you have hearing loss.

So, our hearing aids improve symptoms of tinnitus for our clients by:

Improving overall hearing. By improving the quality of external sound, hearing aids make you less focused on the inner sounds caused by tinnitus.

Retraining the brain. Our hearing aids use a technique called habituation. Habituation uses external sound to teach the brain to ignore tinnitus.

So your brain can eventually turn down the tinnitus by focusing on louder, clearer sounds like people talking around you. Eventually youll notice your tinnitus less.

And this is backed up by data. At least 4 different studies have shown that hearing aids are effective in reducing the effects of tinnitus.

Experts agree : Hearing aids can help with falls and tinnitus.
