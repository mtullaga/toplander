He who is not investing is losing money

To start, let's think: what do you usually do with the money you earn? It seems as if there are only two options: it can be spent or saved. I don’t think it’s worth it to teach you how to spend money. Or how to save either. I’d like to talk about a third option, which most people forget for some reason.

I'm talking about investments: financial investments that over time bring in passive income as interest on the amount invested. In short, it looks like this: you invest your money in a certain type of investment and after a certain period of time you withdraw it along with the interest. Depending on the type of investment, the rate of return can be 5%, 10%, 15%, and in some cases 100%, 200%, and even higher.

Strangely enough, only 5% of the world's population is investing. Every time I become aware of this fact, I feel queasy. Just think of how much money people lose by not knowing or understanding investment tools. I say “lose” because this is another quite surprising fact for many people!

I hope you know about inflation. Thanks to constantly growing inflation, your money depreciates over time. $100 will equal $98 or even $80 after a year – it all depends on the inflation rate.

What can you do to keep your hard-earned money? My answer: invest. The main thing is to determine where. It all depends on the amount of money you have and other factors, which I’ll discuss below. For example, I earn money by trading. In this case, the rate of return is 80% to 92% of the amount invested, and you can withdraw the earned profit within a period of one to five days.

Where you can invest your money

1. Bank deposit

  • Starting amount: depends on the bank, but usually at least $1,000
  • Yield: depends on the bank, but no more than 5%
  • Problems and risks: unable to withdraw money before 6 months
  • Profit waiting period: at least 6 months
  • Summary: this a stable type of investment with a low yield and a relatively long period for making a profit.

2. Foreign currency

  • Starting amount: any, but it’s more profitable to invest more
  • Yield: depends on exchange rate fluctuations, but as a rule, no more than 5-10%
  • Problems and risks: you can go into a serious deficit if the currency loses value, and a sizable profit can be made only if you buy currency in large amounts (at least $10,000)
  • Profit waiting period: unknown, depends on exchange rate fluctuations
  • Summary: an unstable type of investment with income that depends on the size of the initial amount, high risks, and an unpredictable period for making a profit.

3. Real estate

  • Starting amount: depends on the type of real estate, but as a rule, at least $10,000
  • Yield: depends on the type of real estate, but as a rule, 15-20%
  • Problems and risks: real estate needs to be cared for and maintained in good condition. There is the chance it may be lost due to natural disasters or other force majeure
  • Profit waiting period: depends on the type of real estate, but as a rule, 10-20 years
  • Summary: a relatively stable type of investment with an average yield and a very long period for making a profit.

4. Cryptocurrency

  • Starting amount: any, but it’s more profitable to invest more
  • Yield: depends on exchange rate fluctuations, it can reach 100% or higher
  • Problems and risks: illegal in some countries, it’s difficult to forecast the result
  • Profit waiting period: unknown, depends on exchange rate fluctuations
  • Summary: an unstable type of investment with income that depends on the size of the initial amount, high risks, and an unpredictable period for making a profit.

5. Your own business

  • Starting amount: depends on the area of ​​business, but usually at least $1,000
  • Yield: depends on the area of business, as well as the investor’s knowledge and personal traits
  • Problems and risks: it’s difficult to forecast the result, the business may go bust
  • Profit waiting period: depends on the area of ​​business, but usually at least 2-3 years
  • Summary: an unstable type of investment with income that depends on the chosen field and the investor’s personal traits, with high risks and many pitfalls.

6. Trading

  • Starting amount: any, but it’s more profitable to invest more
  • Yield: depends on the broker, for example, 80% to 92% on Olymp Trade
  • Problems and risks: you need at least a minimum understanding of trading
  • Profit waiting period: at least a few minutes
  • Summary: a type of investment with a sufficiently high income and a fast period for making a profit

Want an income of 5% per year or 80% from each successful trade today? Only you can decide

As you can see, the investment types depend only on your desires and abilities. Do you want a stable but small income to cover your losses from inflation? Then put your money into a savings account. But be prepared: you have to wait half a year to withdraw it. If you think that life is fairly unpredictable and you might need money at any moment, then it’s obvious that this option isn’t for you.

It’s entirely possible that you’re a big adventurer and ready to take risks on buying/selling currencies. Of course, I don’t see why you shouldn’t. Just be prepared that one day the exchange rate may soar and you’ll either earn your 10-20% yield or lose and not recover your money. It’s very possible that any cryptocurrency trades in your country will be made illegal, and then your accounts will be disabled and you may even be arrested. Yes, this happens. Serious money means serious risks. I’ve warned you.

Who knows, maybe you decide to buy a home outside the city and, however you look at it, it’s a profitable investment. Real estate always rises in value, and in this case you can settle there or pass it on to your children and grandchildren. Except a nice country home requires serious money both for the purchase and for maintenance. So, bear in mind the additional money needed for maintenance repairs, utilities, and annual insurance against natural disasters and theft. Who knows, maybe in a couple of years, instead of a cozy bedroom community, you’ll have a newly formed ghetto with a nearby nuclear power plant. Anything is possible in this life.

If you ask me which method of earning money I prefer, I’d definitely say trading. Because it’s an opportunity to get 80% to 92% of the invested amount today. Of course, first you have to practice a little to understand how it all works. Here is your main desire; everything else will follow

In general, no matter what anyone says, in this life you decide everything yourself, and both the choice and the responsibility always lie only with you. One person takes risks and earns big money, another person prefers to be content with small but stable income. To each his own. Now, the best part is a proven earning strategy that works

I recommend starting with an “up-down” strategy, as it doesn’t require serious immersion in trading and even beginners can use it.

Step 1. Choose a currency pair

As a rule, EUR/USD works well.

Step 2. Set the time to 1 minute and the amount to $1

If you want to earn more, then make a higher bet. However, I’ll explain the strategy by using this particular minimum bet as an example.

Step 3. Start trading

Everything here is simple – you have to predict whether the exchange rate will rise or fall within 1 minute after opening a trade. Remember that you can make any forecast with this strategy – it doesn’t matter whether you choose UP or DOWN.

Let’s say that you’ve chosen UP

  • If the chart actually goes up, $1.82 will be returned to your account, having made 82 cents in profit

    Then you need to make your next trade, but this time you should choose the opposite value — DOWN. Of course, you can bet with a larger amount if you want to earn more.

  • If the chart goes the other way (down), then your trade was unsuccessful

    This means that you need to raise your next trade to $3 and change the direction of the chart again. So, if your last choice was DOWN, now you need to choose UP.

  • If the chart didn’t go the way you wanted again, then you lose this trade

    But don’t worry. You have nothing to worry about because with this strategy you always make up for all losses. To recoup your losses and make a profit, increase the trade amount to $8 (without changing the trade time) and choose DOWN.

Excellent! You chose the right direction and received $14.56. You not only made up for the loss, but also went into the black by making a profit. Now go back to trading and make a new bet, but this time you need to choose UP. Yes, that’s why it’s called the up-down strategy ;)

Important point

Always change the direction of trades regardless of whether or not your bid was successful. If you want to test your strength, then start with a minimum bet of $1. If you lose, increase the amount to $3. If you lose again, change it to $8. If this happens again, increase the amount to $18. And don’t worry, I’ve personally never had to do this, even though I trade every day. Once a trade is successful, return to the first step and start again. The amount of the bet can be increased because this strategy works for any amount. And don’t forget: the bigger the bet, the bigger the profit!
